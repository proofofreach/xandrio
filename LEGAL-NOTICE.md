Xandrio is self-hosted software. It is not a content provider, book store, hosted TTS service, or project-operated catalog. The operator controls the server, sources, credentials, library, and generated playback cache.

Before importing, downloading, narrating, or sharing access to content, the operator must determine whether they have the rights and other lawful basis required by applicable law and the relevant provider or publisher agreements. Xandrio does not determine copyright, public-domain, licence, or provider-rights status worldwide. Provider metadata can be absent, incomplete, or wrong.

Owning or lawfully accessing an ebook may permit personal reading or text-to-speech use in some jurisdictions, but ownership alone does not answer every question about copying, format shifting, access controls, acquisition from another source, or sharing. Those rules and exceptions vary. Xandrio does not geolocate operators or enable providers by country; each operator selects and configures sources for their own instance.

Xandrio does not include ebook DRM-removal functionality and rejects known DRM-protected Kindle imports. Do not use another tool to bypass a technological protection measure merely to make a file usable in Xandrio. Ownership of an ebook does not, by itself, establish that circumvention or acquisition of another copy is permitted. The optional Anna browser integration changes automation fingerprints; it is disabled by default and requires a separate operator setting after review of the provider's current terms.

Anna's Archive, Z-Library, and other sources whose rights status is unverified are optional and disabled on a new instance until the operator separately enables them. Enabling a connector is a technical setting, not a finding that a provider, work, or intended use is lawful. See [docs/CONNECTOR_POLICY.md](docs/CONNECTOR_POLICY.md).

Generated audio is intended for the operator's private playback unless the operator has broader rights. Xandrio has no social-sharing or audiobook-publishing feature.

If an operator supplies a voice reference, they must have authority to use it and must obtain any required consent. Voice references and generated audio remain under the operator's control.
